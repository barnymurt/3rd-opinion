# Bias Detection Domain

Identifies cognitive biases, framing issues, and potential blind spots in AI responses.

## What to Look For

### Cognitive Biases

**Confirmation Bias**
- Cherry-picking evidence
- Dismissing counter-examples
- One-sided presentation

**Authority Bias**
- Accepting claims without verification
- Appeal to popularity
- "Everyone knows" framing

**Anchoring Bias**
- First impression dominates
- Initial information overvalued

**Availability Heuristic**
- Recent events overrepresented
- Dramatic events given undue weight

**Status Quo Bias**
- Change presented as risky
- Current approach favored

**Framing Effects**
- Same info, different presentation = different conclusions
- Positive vs negative framing
- Loss vs gain framing

### Framing Issues
- Loaded language
- Emotional appeals
- Selective presentation
- False equivalences

## Trigger Indicators

Look for:
- Strong emotional language
- One-sided presentations
- Absolutes and generalizations
- Appeal to authority or popularity
- Loss-avoidance framing
- Selective facts

## Analysis Questions

1. How is this information being framed?
2. What emotions is the response trying to evoke?
3. What is not being said?
4. Who benefits from this presentation?
5. What alternative framings exist?
6. What biases might affect this response?

## Domain Weight Factors

Increase when:
- Response makes strong claims
- Uses emotional language
- Presents one side
- Uses absolutes

Decrease when:
- Balanced presentation
- Appropriate hedging
- Acknowledges limitations
- Presents alternatives
