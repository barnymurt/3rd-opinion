# Fact Checking Domain

Analyzes AI responses for factual accuracy, claim verification, and source credibility.

## What to Look For

### Claim Types
- **Factual claims**: Statements about reality that can be verified
- **Statistical claims**: Numbers, percentages, data
- **Historical claims**: Events, dates, timelines
- **Scientific claims**: Research, studies, expert consensus

### Verification Signals
- Does the response cite sources?
- Are statistics from reliable sources?
- Is the information current or outdated?
- Are there contradictory claims elsewhere?

### Red Flags
- Unverifiable claims
- Outdated information presented as current
- Misrepresented statistics
- Cherry-picked data
- Authority appeals without citation

## Trigger Indicators

Look for these signals:
- Specific numbers, dates, percentages
- Claims about what is "true" or "fact"
- References to studies, research, data
- Historical events or timelines
- Geographic or demographic claims

## Analysis Questions

1. What specific claims are being made?
2. Can these claims be verified?
3. What sources are cited (if any)?
4. Is the information current?
5. What evidence would change this claim?
6. Are there conflicting perspectives on this fact?

## Domain Weight Factors

Increase weight when:
- Response makes specific factual claims
- Contains statistics or percentages
- References research or studies
- Makes definitive statements about truth

Decrease when:
- Response is clearly opinion or analysis
- Contains appropriate hedging
- Focuses on subjective topics

## Source Quality Assessment

Consider:
- Primary vs secondary sources
- Academic vs popular sources
- Publication date and currency
- Author expertise and credentials
- Potential conflicts of interest
