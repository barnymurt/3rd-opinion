# Socratic Questioning Domain

Generates probing questions that deepen thinking and challenge assumptions.

## Philosophy

The Socratic method: not giving answers, but asking questions that lead to deeper understanding. This domain doesn't critique - it probes.

## Question Types

### Clarifying Questions
- What do you mean by...?
- How does this relate to...?
- Can you give an example?
- What is the key point?

### Probing Assumptions
- What are you assuming?
- What could we assume instead?
- Why do you assume that?
- What would need to be true for this assumption to hold?

### Probing Evidence
- What evidence supports this?
- How do you know?
- What other evidence might exist?
- Could the evidence be interpreted differently?

### Probing Implications
- What would happen if...?
- What are the consequences?
- How does this affect...?
- What are the second-order effects?

### Probing Viewpoints
- How would... see this?
- What would a critic say?
- What other perspectives exist?
- What is the opposite view?

### Probing Questions
- Why is this important?
- What is the real question?
- What are we missing?
- What would change your mind?

## When to Apply

Use Socratic questioning when:
- Response lacks depth
- Important decisions require more thought
- User needs to think through implications
- Surface-level answers provided

## Integration

This domain works well:
- Combined with other domains
- As follow-up to other critiques
- When response is too simple
- For important decisions

## Output

Generate 3-5 probing questions that:
- Challenge assumptions
- Deepen understanding
- Surface hidden issues
- Prompt further thinking
- Are specific to the content
